# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two equal audiences:

**Event planners and bookers** — corporate event planners, conference organizers, school and church bookers. They arrive with a decision to make: is David the right speaker for our event? They need credentials fast, a sense of his energy, and a clear path to inquiry. Friction kills the conversion.

**Fans and supporters** — college football fans, ESPN viewers, people who follow David's faith and leadership content. They're here to connect with someone they already trust. They want the book, the podcast, the latest content, and a reason to stay in his orbit.

## Product Purpose

David Pollack's personal brand hub. Generates speaking inquiries, sells the book, and builds an audience around his faith-and-football perspective on leadership and life. Success means a booker fills out the inquiry form, a fan buys the book or subscribes, and both leave with a stronger sense of who David is.

## Brand Personality

Competitor. Believer. Real.

Athletic energy meets genuine conviction. Not corporate, not hype. The intersection of the guy who played with reckless abandon and the man who knows what actually matters. Bold enough to stop a scroll. Grounded enough to earn trust.

## Anti-references

**Generic motivational speaker site** — the primary anti-reference. Stock photos of crowds, gradient text over hero images, "INSPIRE | MOTIVATE | TRANSFORM" stacked in giant letters, testimonial carousels, the cheesy booking CTA with a stock handshake photo. If it looks like it could belong to any speaker on the circuit, it has failed.

**ESPN / sports broadcast UI** — score tickers, live-data feel, split-screen broadcast layouts. This is David's personal site, not a network property.

**Corporate keynote circuit** — overly polished, suit-and-tie brochure feel. Booking agency pages, conference PDFs turned into websites.

**Megachurch / faith influencer aesthetic** — pastel backgrounds, hand-lettered scripture quotes, softened photography. Faith is central to David's identity but it's one dimension, not the whole visual language.

## Design Principles

1. **Credentials show, copy doesn't tell.** Hall of Famer. 3x All-American. Lott Trophy. Bednarik Award. 7x Emmy. 12 years ESPN. Let the facts carry the authority — remove copy that explains why those facts matter.

2. **One person, not a brand machine.** The site should feel like David — direct, competitive, honest. Not a polished agency product. Every design decision should ask: does this feel like a person or a package?

3. **Booker path is always visible.** A booking decision can happen at any scroll depth. The speaking inquiry path should never be more than one click away from any page.

4. **Energy without noise.** High energy doesn't mean crowded. The best football players make the game look effortless. The design should have the same quality — intensity through restraint, not decoration.

5. **Faith runs underneath, not on top.** It informs the tone (conviction, gratitude, purpose) without dominating the visual language. Someone who doesn't share the faith should still feel completely welcome.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. High color contrast given the dark palette. Reduced-motion support for scroll-reveal animations. All images require descriptive alt text — no exceptions.
